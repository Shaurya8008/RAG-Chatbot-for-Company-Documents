# Acme Corporation Code of Conduct
**Version 1.8 | Effective Date: January 1, 2025 | Department: Legal & Compliance**
**Access Level: All Employees | Owner: Chief Compliance Officer**

---

## 1. Purpose

This Code of Conduct establishes the ethical standards and behavioral expectations for all Acme Corporation employees, contractors, and partners. Adherence to this code is a condition of employment.

## 2. Core Values

Acme Corporation operates on four core values:
1. **Integrity**: We act honestly and transparently in all business dealings
2. **Respect**: We treat every person with dignity and fairness
3. **Innovation**: We encourage creative thinking and responsible experimentation
4. **Accountability**: We take ownership of our work and its impact

## 3. Ethical Business Practices

### 3.1 Anti-Bribery and Corruption
Employees must not offer, give, solicit, or accept bribes, kickbacks, or improper payments of any kind. This includes gifts to government officials, facilitation payments, and any payment intended to influence a business decision.

Gift exchanges with business partners must not exceed $100 in value and must be reported to the compliance team.

### 3.2 Fair Competition
Acme competes fairly and ethically. Employees must not:
- Engage in price-fixing or market allocation with competitors
- Share confidential pricing or strategy information with competitors
- Make false or misleading claims about competitors' products or services

### 3.3 Insider Trading
Employees with access to material non-public information about Acme or its business partners must not trade securities based on that information or share it with others who might trade on it.

### 3.4 Record Keeping
All business records must be accurate, complete, and maintained in accordance with company retention policies. Falsifying records or destroying documents subject to legal hold is strictly prohibited and may result in criminal penalties.

## 4. Workplace Behavior

### 4.1 Harassment-Free Workplace
Acme maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment, including:
- Sexual harassment (unwelcome advances, requests for favors, verbal or physical conduct)
- Bullying and intimidation
- Discrimination based on protected characteristics
- Hostile work environment behaviors

### 4.2 Reporting Harassment
If you experience or witness harassment:
1. Document the incident (date, time, location, witnesses)
2. Report to your manager, HR, or the Ethics Hotline
3. Reports can be made anonymously
4. Retaliation against reporters is strictly prohibited and will result in disciplinary action

### 4.3 Substance Abuse
Acme is a drug-free workplace. Employees must not:
- Use illegal drugs on company premises or during work hours
- Be under the influence of alcohol or drugs while performing work duties
- Possess or distribute controlled substances at work

Moderate alcohol consumption at company-sponsored events is permitted for employees of legal drinking age.

### 4.4 Violence Prevention
Threats, intimidation, and acts of violence are prohibited. Employees must report any threatening behavior to their manager and Security immediately.

## 5. Information and Data Protection

### 5.1 Confidential Information
Employees must protect all confidential company information, including:
- Trade secrets and proprietary technology
- Financial data and business strategies
- Customer and vendor data
- Employee personal information
- Unpublished product plans

### 5.2 Data Privacy
Acme complies with all applicable data privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, etc.). Employees must:
- Collect only necessary personal data
- Use personal data only for stated business purposes
- Protect personal data with appropriate security measures
- Report data breaches to the Privacy team within 24 hours

### 5.3 Intellectual Property
All work product created during employment belongs to Acme Corporation as outlined in the employment agreement. Employees must not use company IP for personal gain or share it with unauthorized parties.

## 6. Environmental and Social Responsibility

### 6.1 Environmental Commitment
Acme is committed to minimizing environmental impact. Employees should:
- Reduce, reuse, and recycle in the workplace
- Use energy-efficient practices
- Minimize unnecessary travel and printing
- Report environmental concerns to the Facilities team

### 6.2 Community Engagement
Acme encourages community involvement through volunteer time off (2 days per year) and the company matching program for charitable donations up to $500 per employee per year.

## 7. Compliance and Enforcement

### 7.1 Reporting Violations
Employees must report suspected violations of this Code through any of these channels:
- Direct manager
- HR Department (hr@acmecorp.example.com)
- Ethics Hotline (ethics@acmecorp.example.com) — anonymous reporting available
- Chief Compliance Officer

### 7.2 Investigation Process
All reports are investigated promptly and confidentially. The investigation process typically takes 10-15 business days and includes:
1. Initial assessment and documentation
2. Interviews with relevant parties
3. Evidence review
4. Findings and recommendations
5. Resolution and follow-up

### 7.3 Disciplinary Actions
Violations of this Code may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Required retraining
- Suspension without pay
- Demotion or reassignment
- Termination of employment
- Legal action where appropriate

The severity of disciplinary action depends on the nature and circumstances of the violation.

### 7.4 Annual Acknowledgment
All employees must acknowledge and sign this Code of Conduct annually during the compliance training period in Q1. Failure to complete the acknowledgment may affect performance reviews and continued employment eligibility.

---

*This Code of Conduct applies to all Acme Corporation employees, contractors, and partners worldwide. Questions should be directed to compliance@acmecorp.example.com. Last updated: January 1, 2025.*
