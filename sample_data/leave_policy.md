# Acme Corporation Leave Policy
**Version 2.1 | Effective Date: January 1, 2025 | Department: Human Resources**
**Access Level: All Employees | Owner: HR Department**

---

## 1. Overview

This policy outlines the types of leave available to Acme Corporation employees, eligibility requirements, and procedures for requesting time off.

## 2. Paid Time Off (PTO)

### 2.1 PTO Accrual Rates

| Tenure | Annual PTO Days | Accrual Rate (per month) |
|---|---|---|
| 0–1 years | 15 days | 1.25 days |
| 1–3 years | 18 days | 1.50 days |
| 3–5 years | 22 days | 1.83 days |
| 5–10 years | 25 days | 2.08 days |
| 10+ years | 30 days | 2.50 days |

### 2.2 PTO Carryover
Employees may carry over up to 5 unused PTO days to the following calendar year. Carryover days must be used by March 31. Days beyond the carryover limit will be forfeited on December 31.

### 2.3 PTO Request Process
1. Submit PTO requests through the HR portal at least 5 business days in advance
2. Requests of 5 or more consecutive days require 2 weeks advance notice
3. Manager approval is required for all PTO requests
4. Requests during blackout periods (month-end close, annual planning) may be denied based on business needs

## 3. Sick Leave

### 3.1 Sick Leave Allowance
All full-time employees receive 10 paid sick days per year, accrued at approximately 0.83 days per month. Part-time employees receive a prorated amount.

### 3.2 Usage
Sick leave may be used for:
- Personal illness or injury
- Medical appointments (doctor, dentist, specialist)
- Mental health days
- Caring for an immediate family member who is ill

### 3.3 Documentation
A doctor's note is required for absences of 3 or more consecutive sick days. For extended illnesses (5+ days), employees should contact HR to discuss FMLA eligibility.

## 4. Company Holidays

Acme Corporation observes the following paid holidays:

| Holiday | Date |
|---|---|
| New Year's Day | January 1 |
| Martin Luther King Jr. Day | Third Monday in January |
| Presidents' Day | Third Monday in February |
| Memorial Day | Last Monday in May |
| Independence Day | July 4 |
| Labor Day | First Monday in September |
| Thanksgiving Day | Fourth Thursday in November |
| Day After Thanksgiving | Fourth Friday in November |
| Christmas Eve | December 24 |
| Christmas Day | December 25 |
| New Year's Eve (half day) | December 31 |

When a holiday falls on a Saturday, it is observed on the preceding Friday. When it falls on a Sunday, it is observed on the following Monday.

## 5. Parental Leave

### 5.1 Birth Parent Leave
Birth parents receive 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave. An additional 4 weeks of unpaid leave may be requested.

### 5.2 Non-Birth Parent Leave
Non-birth parents (including adoptive and foster parents) receive 8 weeks of fully paid parental leave.

### 5.3 Eligibility
Parental leave is available to employees who have completed at least 6 months of continuous employment.

### 5.4 Return to Work
Employees returning from parental leave are guaranteed their same or equivalent position. A gradual return schedule (part-time for the first 2 weeks) may be arranged with manager approval.

## 6. Bereavement Leave

Employees receive paid bereavement leave as follows:
- **Immediate family** (spouse, child, parent, sibling): 5 days
- **Extended family** (grandparent, grandchild, in-law): 3 days
- **Close friend or colleague**: 1 day

Additional unpaid time may be granted at manager discretion.

## 7. Jury Duty

Employees summoned for jury duty will receive full pay for up to 10 business days. For extended jury service beyond 10 days, the company will pay the difference between jury duty compensation and the employee's regular salary.

## 8. Unpaid Leave of Absence

Employees who have exhausted PTO and sick leave may request an unpaid leave of absence for up to 30 days. Requests must be submitted to HR in writing and require department head approval. Benefits continue during approved unpaid leave up to 30 days.

## 9. FMLA Leave

Eligible employees (12+ months of service, 1,250+ hours worked) may take up to 12 weeks of job-protected unpaid leave per year under the Family and Medical Leave Act for qualifying reasons including serious health conditions, family care, and military family leave.
